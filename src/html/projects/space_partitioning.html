Space Partitioning

This is a collection of acceleration data structures I have implemented in various graphics projects. Some were used to optimize rendered scene objects, while others helped minimize intersection checks with complex meshes.

Bounding Volume Hierarchy (BVH)

BVH is a tree structure where each node represents a bounding volume that encloses a subset of objects. It's commonly used in ray tracing and collision detection to quickly discard large groups of objects that donâ€™t intersect with a ray or volume.

Octrees

Octrees recursively subdivide 3D space into eight octants. They are especially useful in scenes with large empty spaces.

KD-trees

KD-trees are binary trees that split space along alternating axes. They are used for nearest neighbor searches and ray tracing.
